Sourdough Bread

Crusty, Rustic Sourdough

Imagine sinking your teeth into a loaf of freshly baked sourdough, where the deep, crackling crust gives way to a tender, airy interior. The aroma of natural fermentation fills the room, mingling with subtle notes of tang and earthiness that hint at a slow, artisanal process. Each slice reveals a mosaic of delicate bubbles, the perfect balance of rustic charm and modern craftsmanship, promising a delightful chew and a burst of flavor in every bite.

This sourdough masterpiece is not merely bread—it's an experience. Its complex flavor profile, with a whisper of acidity and the warmth of a golden crust, invites you to savor every moment. Whether enjoyed on its own, drizzled with olive oil, or paired with your favorite spread, each mouthful transports you to a cozy bakery filled with tradition and passion, making every meal an indulgent celebration of taste.

  • 500g bread flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 350g water (room temperature)
  • 100g active sourdough starter (fed and bubbly)
  • 10g salt

Steps

  1. Autolyse

    • In a large bowl, combine the flour and water. Mix until no dry spots remain, cover, and let it rest for 30 minutes. This process hydrates the flour and develops gluten.

  2. Mix in Starter & Salt:

    • Add the active sourdough starter and salt. Mix well until fully incorporated. You can use your hands to gently fold and knead the dough until it’s smooth.

  3. Bulk Fermentation & Stretch & Folds:

    • Cover the bowl with a damp towel or plastic wrap. Let the dough rest at room temperature for about 4–5 hours. During the first 2 hours, perform a set of stretch and folds every 30 minutes (4 sets total) to strengthen the dough.

  4. Pre-Shape & Rest:

    • Once the bulk fermentation is complete, gently turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and pre-shape it into a loose ball. Let it rest for 20–30 minutes.

  5. Final Shape & Proof:

    • Shape the dough into a tighter ball or oval. Place it seam-side up in a well-floured proofing basket (banneton). Cover and let it proof at room temperature for 1–2 hours, or for better flavor and structure, refrigerate overnight (8–12 hours).

  6. Preheat & Prepare for Baking:

    • About 30 minutes before baking, preheat your oven to 500°F (260°C) with a Dutch oven (or heavy covered pot) inside. This creates the steam needed for a crispy crust.

  7. Score & Bake:

    • Carefully transfer your cold dough onto a piece of parchment paper. Gently flip it so the seam side is down, then score the top with a sharp knife or lame. Lower the oven temperature to 450°F (230°C), place the dough (with the parchment) into the Dutch oven, cover, and bake for 20 minutes.

  8. Finish Baking:

    • Remove the lid, and continue baking for an additional 20–25 minutes until the crust is a deep golden brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

  9. Cool & Enjoy:

    • Transfer the bread to a cooling rack and let it cool completely before slicing. This final rest allows the crumb to set and the flavors to mature.
